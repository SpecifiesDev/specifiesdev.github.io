    Questions

    Did Gatsby achieve the American dream?

    Ultimately, Gatsby did achieve the reality of what is perceived as the American dream. He was born to a dirt poor farming family, and rose to be one of the most promiment rich people in New York. That's the concept of the American dream. While it collapsed around him due to a series of unfortunate events, Gatsby did what so many could not and achieved the American dream.


    What did Daisy love? Gatsby, money, or herself?

    I think Daisy truly loved Gatsby. Many examples can be found throughout the novel. For example, the first luncheon with Daisy and her husband Tom, Nick Carraway happens to mention Gatsby and Daisy's immediate response is one that draws her complete attention. Most women wouldn't do this to someone they had relations with five years prior. The counter-argument would be that maybe she was gasping because she knew of his money, and Tom was a terrible husband. This wouldn't work, because when Daisy met him, he was an attending officer to one of her family's parties. She was aware that he didn't come from money.

    What made Gatsby's dream corrupt?

    His love for Daisy. His love for Daisy led to him making questionable choices in business practice, associating him with terrible people like Wolfsheim. He didn't climb the ladder in a legitimate sense.
