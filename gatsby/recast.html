    • In personal opinion, the casting of Isla Fisher as Myrtle in the Great Gatsby was an apt choice. She's an attractive woman, who has an open personality, perfect for the mistress role that Myrtle plays. For example, the book describes her as a "full fit" woman. A woman who draws the attention of the men around her. With that being said, the casting doesn't really reflect the class of Myrtle being a poor wife to a man who lives in the Valley of Ashes. Isla Fisher would be fit to play an upper class woman, or even a middle woman. This is why I chose Emma Stone to replace her. She has a stunning elegance to her figure, while being able to fit into the role of an impoverished wife.

    Tobey Maguire is the absolute perfect pick to represent The Great Gatsby's Nick Carraway. He has the perfect way of carrying himself that shows the care that he puts into the people around him, regardless of their class. He also helps portray Nick as understanding people. Since Tobey Maguire gives off an understanding nature, we can see him in great comparison to the character when Carraway states "No amount of fire or freshness can challenge what a man will store up his ghostly heart." (Page 103) This is why I will choose to keep Toby Maguire on the cast, he's perfectly fit for the role.

    I'll have to say that Carry Mulligan was the perfect pick to play Daisy, considering the era that the book was written in. She just emanates a beauty that is commonly seen in the women of the 20's. Yet, the book portrays her as a woman who comes from upper-class society. We see this multiple times throughout the novel when her heritage is described to be of a patrician family. This statement is why I will choose Natalie Portman to replace her in the casting. Portman is undeniably beautiful, but also gives off an emanation that displays her as "royalty." Thus, making her the perfect match for the primary traits of Daisy.
