    www.bbc.com/culture/story/20161004-did-the-20s-really-roar.

    Dorr, Lisa Lindquist. “Why Prohibition Failed.” We're History, 18 Dec. 2015,
    werehistory.org/why-prohibition-failed/.
    The article begins by explaining why prohibition initially occured. It goes into the temperance movement had pushed for prohibition. They essentially cited a lack of morality, destruction of happiness, swelling of prisons, and many other social issues. It also describes how their idea of an alcoholic free utopian society was not realistic, especially in concerns of the time period. The article effectively explains why most controversial ban suggestions never occur - the black market. If you ban a highly demanded commodity, you're effectively going to open up a network of underground criminal activity. This source will be effective in explaining why the prohibition was a misplaced concept, why it failed, and how its failure helped shape the contemporary American identity.


    Gidlow, Liette. “Beyond 1920: The Legacies of Woman Suffrage (U.S. National Park Service).” National Parks Service, Department of the Interior, 5 Apr. 2019,
    www.nps.gov/articles/beyond-1920-the-legacies-of-woman-suffrage.htm.
    This source aptly describes the struggle for suffrage and how it affected contemporary society and inevitably rippled into the modern socio-political world. It speaks of how pioneers of women's suffrage had challenged the normal polite view for nearly a century. It also describes the struggle that women would face even after they gained the right to vote. Much like the African Americans after the civil war, it took time for society to accept the law. This source will allow an explanation of the political strife of the time period, and how it shaped America.
